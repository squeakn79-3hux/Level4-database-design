# Introduction

This document outlines the business justification for developing the Inventory Management Database System for Bright Sparks Ltd. It identifies the operational problems experienced by the business, evaluates the impact of those problems and explains how the proposed database solution addresses them.

The objective is to demonstrate that implementing a relational database provides measurable improvements to business operations while supporting future growth.

---

# Current Situation

Bright Sparks Ltd currently relies on manual processes to manage products, suppliers and sales information. As the volume of inventory and customer transactions increases, these processes become increasingly inefficient and prone to error.

Manual record keeping makes it difficult to maintain accurate inventory levels, identify sales trends and produce reliable reports. The business also faces unnecessary duplication of information and increased administration time when updating records.

Without a structured database system, business information becomes fragmented and difficult to manage effectively.

---

# Problems Identified

The following business issues were identified during the planning phase:

- Manual entry of inventory information.
- Duplicate records across business processes.
- Difficulty monitoring stock levels.
- Limited reporting capabilities.
- Slow retrieval of supplier and product information.
- Increased likelihood of human error.
- Lack of real-time visibility of inventory performance.

Collectively, these issues reduce productivity and limit the ability of management to make informed business decisions.

---

# Proposed Solution

The proposed solution is the implementation of a relational database developed using MySQL with a user-friendly interface created in LibreOffice Base.

The system provides a central location for storing business information while enforcing relationships between products, suppliers and sales records through a structured database design.

Users can efficiently maintain inventory information, record sales transactions and generate reports without relying on manual record-keeping methods.

---

# Expected Business Benefits

The proposed database system delivers several operational benefits, including:

- Improved accuracy of business records.
- Reduced duplication of data.
- Faster retrieval of information.
- Improved inventory visibility.
- Better supplier management.
- More effective sales reporting.
- Increased staff productivity.
- Improved business decision-making.

The solution also provides a scalable foundation capable of supporting future business expansion.

---

# Costs and Considerations

As this project was developed as part of a simulated business scenario, implementation costs have not been formally calculated.

In a real-world deployment, considerations would include:

- Database server infrastructure.
- Staff training.
- Data migration.
- Ongoing maintenance.
- System backups.
- Security and user management.

These factors would form part of a detailed implementation plan before deployment.

---

# Recommendation

Based on the identified business requirements and operational challenges, the implementation of the Inventory Management Database System is recommended.

The proposed solution addresses the limitations of manual record keeping while improving data integrity, operational efficiency and reporting capabilities.

The project demonstrates how a well-designed relational database can provide long-term value by supporting accurate information management and informed business decision-making.

---

# Conclusion

The business case demonstrates that investing in a structured relational database provides significant benefits to Bright Sparks Ltd.

By replacing manual processes with a centralised database solution, the business gains improved efficiency, better reporting capabilities and greater confidence in the accuracy of its operational data.
